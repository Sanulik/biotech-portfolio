04. Bio Pulse: Formal Roundtable Discussion

Assignment Description

  • Topic: Human Germline Editing (CRISPR): Should we permit the permanent alteration of the human genome to eliminate disease and enhance traits?
  • Role: Moderator
  • Format: Formal Roundtable Discussion titled "Bio Pulse"
  • Key Content: This discussion brought together a Transhumanist, a Clinical Geneticist, an FDA Regulator, and a Disability Rights Advocate to navigate the heartbeat of the future. As the moderator, I facilitated a debate on the technical volatility of CRISPR-Cas9, the ethics of "erasing" disability, and the regulatory challenges of preventing a genetic class divide.

Summary of My Role & Spoken Parts

As the moderator, my primary goal was to facilitate a rigorous debate that integrated opposing stakeholder viewpoints into a unified Consensus Statement, ensuring policy prioritizes public safety and ethical integrity. I acted as an advocate for deliberative democracy, remaining neutral to bridge the gap between radical visionaries and pragmatic realists.

Key Contributions during the Discussion:

  • Technical Scrutiny: I challenged the Transhumanist on the risks of Mosaicism and the ethics of "gambling" with children's lives.
  • Ethical Inquiry: I pushed the stakeholders to consider the "human cost" of editing, questioning if the intent is moral even if the science becomes perfect.
  • Policy Facilitation: I addressed the risk of creating a "Genetic Elite," prompting the FDA regulator to discuss the consolidation of patented biological interventions.
  • Consensus Building: I successfully led the group to agree on a moratorium on germline editing until safety is proven (specifically less than 0.01% off-target mutations) and restricted use to therapeutic purposes only.
